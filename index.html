Project Overview

The Fishing Game is an innovative educational toy crafted entirely from recycled materials. Designed for children aged 3-7 years, this interactive game combines principles of sustainability with STEM learning through engaging magnetic fishing gameplay.

Core Objectives:

  • Environmental Education: Demonstrate creative reuse of common household waste materials
  • Motor Skill Development: Enhance hand-eye coordination and fine motor control
  • Cognitive Growth: Teach color/number recognition and basic magnetic principles
  • Affordable Design: Create durable play equipment with less than $10 in materials

Game Mechanics:

The system consists of:

  • A 40×60cm cardboard "fishing pool" with painted aquatic design
  • 5-10 magnetic fish crafted from recycled medicine bottles
  • 2-4 fishing rods with magnetic tips
  • Scoring system using numbered fish (optional)

When the magnetic tip of the rod comes within 2-3cm of a fish's mouth magnet, they attract, allowing children to "catch" the fish.

Materials Specification

Primary Components:

  • Corrugated Cardboard

    Source: Shipping boxes or packaging material

    Preparation: Flattened, cleaned, and laminated with clear tape for water resistance

    Dimensions: 40×60cm base with 5cm side walls

  • Medicine Bottles (Ampoules)

    Type: Small glass or plastic liquid medicine containers (50ml capacity)

    Quantity: 6-10 units, preferably with cylindrical bodies

    Preparation: Thoroughly cleaned and labels removed

  • Neodymium Magnets

    For Fish: 5mm diameter disc magnets (10 pieces)

    For Rods: 15mm diameter ceramic magnets (2 pieces)

    Safety: Fully encapsulated in food-grade silicone

Construction Supplies:

  • Art Materials

    Non-toxic acrylic paints (blue, green, red, yellow, white)

    Water-based polyurethane varnish

    Permanent markers for detailing

  • Tools

    Utility knife with safety lock

    Blunt-tip scissors

    Low-temperature hot glue gun

    Fine-grit sandpaper (220 grit)

    Paintbrushes (various sizes)

Step-by-Step Construction Guide

Phase 1: Fishing Pool Assembly (2 hours)

  1. Base Construction:
    • Measure and mark 40×60cm rectangle on cardboard
    • Cut using utility knife with metal ruler guide
    • Sand all edges to smooth roughness
  2. Side Walls:
    • Cut 5cm wide strips for perimeter walls
    • Score fold lines every 10cm for smooth corners
    • Attach with hot glue, reinforcing corners
  3. Finishing:
    • Apply blue gradient paint (dark to light)
    • Add wave patterns with lighter blue
    • Seal with 2 coats water-based varnish

Phase 2: Magnetic Fish Creation (3 hours)

  1. Fish Design:
    • Cut medicine bottles into fish shapes (avg. 8cm length)
    • Create 5 distinct species (angelfish, clownfish, etc.)
    • Sand all edges to safety standards
  2. Magnet Installation:
    • Mark magnet position at fish "mouth"
    • Secure neodymium magnet with epoxy resin
    • Allow 1 hour curing time before painting
  3. Painting:
    • Prime with white acrylic base
    • Paint species-appropriate color patterns
    • Number fish 1-5 for scoring system
    • Apply protective varnish coat

Phase 3: Fishing Rod Production (1 hour)

  1. Rod Assembly:
    • Cut 30cm wooden dowels for handles
    • Attach 50cm nylon string with secure knot
  2. Magnetic Tip:
    • Embed ceramic magnet in silicone putty
    • Attach to string end with double knot
    • Seal connection with hot glue
  3. Customization:
    • Color-code handles for multiple players
    • Add grip texture with rubber bands
    • Test magnetic strength with all fish

Advanced Customization Options:

  • Aquatic Decor: Add EVA coral reefs or seaweed
  • Special Fish: Create "golden fish" worth extra points
  • Obstacles: Floating bottle cap "rocks"
  • Storage: Fabric pouch for fish organization

Safety Engineering

This project exceeds international toy safety standards (EN71, ASTM F963) through these design features:

Edge Safety

All cardboard and plastic edges rounded to >1mm radius. Double-checked with edge radius gauge.

Magnet Security

Neodymium magnets encased in 2mm thick silicone sleeves, requiring >5lbs force to remove.

Choking Hazard Prevention

All components exceed 5cm minimum dimension requirement for under-3 safety.

Non-Toxic Materials

AP-certified paints and water-based finishes meeting CPSIA lead content limits.

Usage Guidelines

  • Age Recommendation: 3+ years with supervision, 5+ independent play
  • Inspection: Weekly magnet security checks
  • Cleaning: Damp cloth only - no immersion
  • Storage: Dry environment away from electronics

Technical Challenges Solved

Magnet Strength Optimization

Issue: Initial magnets either too weak (failed to connect) or too strong (difficult for small hands to separate)

Solution: Tested 8 magnet types before selecting ideal 350 Gauss strength

Surface Durability

Issue: Paint chipping during playtesting

Solution: Implemented 3-step finishing process (primer, paint, varnish)
